# Crafting Your Character
_What you were is rarely what you remain..._

## Who Are You Now?
Players will rely on their character sheets to help them understand their motivations, their fears, and the tools they have at hand to solve problems. These details will shift as play progresses, however, by choice or by force. This is an **essential** part of the game. Exploring the Deep Woods changes you, and figuring out how to manage these changes teaches us something about our true selves. Players must respect the words written on their character sheets, even if they're a bit nonsensical, and do their best to try to play the character that is in front of them. Not the character they started with, not the one they *want*, but the character as its written on the sheet. Agreeing to this is the very first bargain a player will make as they step foot in the Deep Woods.

### Details Matter, But Which Ones?
Above each prompt on the Character Sheet is a small description about the kinds of details that should be entered. Agree with your fellow players on a tone and level of detail that you all feel comfortable adhering to, then enter one or two sentences at most for each prompt (except your names, those can just be names). You don't need to write in complete sentences, as long as your intent is clear, but do use plenty of descriptive words (nouns and verbs). You don't need to write "I need" or "I fear", just start by writing the most important details. If you need inspiration, take a look at the following example of a simple character, a naive lad named Fletcher who aims to impress the barber's daughter. 

### Example Character
1. **Ekename**: `Fletcher` *(Profession-names are always recommended, in addition to descriptive-leaning names like "Tall Oak" or "Stoutarm".)*
2. **Truename**: `Syrus` *(A proper name, one you hold close and tell only to your closest companions, if you can help it.)*
3. **What You Need**: `I'll impress Virginia by bringing her a feather from the Bird of Paradise that lives among these trees.` *(Something definitive, achievable even if outlandish, and most importantly something you can call back to time and again with narrative hooks.)*
4. **What You Have**: `A sling and a small bag of stones. A chipped knife, wrapped in bright ribbon. A dirty cloak wrapped around a particularly large sandwich. An impish and child-like sense of curiosity.` *(This section can be more thorough than the others. These are the tools you'll use to solve specific problems.)*
5. **What You Fear**: `Bats and bugs - yuck.` *(Fletcher is a child with childhood fears, but they can also be more existential. Some people fear being alone, others fear failure, and others still fear that which they cannot explain.)*
6. **What You Were**: `An apprentice arrow-smith and mischievous child.` *(These can encompass broader groups of talents, but should be limited to just one or two things. Even the elderly only have time for so many occupations in a single life. These kennings serve as a short-hand for what kinds of problems you feel equipped to take on, and which would be hopelessly outside of your lived experience.)* 
7. **What You Become**: `LEFT BLANK` *(This field will be populated as you play. No one knows what they'll become until they become it!).*

### Changing Details
As play progresses, players will need to edit their character sheets in specific ways. Exploring the Deep Woods has many strange consequences, and among them is losing oneself to the ancient forces that inhabit the trees. Players will be asked to perform the following adjustments over the course of a game, and must use the **Carve New Fate** button to facilitate the changes:
<br><br>
* **[ABANDON]**: The most simple adjustment, simply remove the detail in question. Highlight the text you need to abandon, and do not enter any new text when you _carve new fate_, simply _confirm your fate_ and the text will be crossed out.
* **[BIND]**: The situation asks a player to add a new detail to their character sheet, and players will be told the _exact word(s)_ they need to bind. Do not highlight text, simply place the cursor in a free area and _carve new fate_, then enter the exact words before you _confirm your fate_.
* **[CHANGE]**: In this case, players must replace one or more words on their character sheet with new ones. Sometimes the situation will call for an explicit replacement (a _this for that_ prompt), but other times players can choose any new detail to replace the old. Just as before, highlight the string of text you want to change, then _carve new fate_ and enter the new value before you _confirm new fate_.
* **"Something" / "Not"**: Sometimes, players will be asked to edit "something", which means they are free to pick any prompt on their character sheet as a place to make the required change. Other times, players will be instructed with the format "not [PROMPT]", which means they can select any other prompt as the place to make the required change.
<br>

If the player is making a change to their character sheet as part of a Bargain with a Patron, they must remember to enter a number of *dice claimed in exchange* while they *carve new fate*. It would be foolish to bargain away bits of you for nothing in return.

### Becoming Something New...
Occasionally, players will be instructed to edit [WHAT YOU BECOME], but very rarely. The most probable way this prompt will be updated is by players rolling lots of dice. Every time a player rolls any number of "3s" when casting dice, they'll experience a *Transformative Moment*, and will need to perform a check to see if their character **Becomes** something new. When filling in a Transformative Moment, if there is a [MARK] below the box, players must select the suit of the Location they are currently in. To perform the check, the player rolls 1d6 (this dice is not lost from their pool) - if the rolled value is less than the number of filled in boxes, that character Becomes something new and is offered a choice of things to become. Otherwise, play continues until the next time a character experiences a Transformative Moment. 
<br><br>
Once a character Becomes something new, those details will automatically be moved to the characters [WHAT YOU BECOME] prompt (IRL ONLY - you'll have to do this part yourself!). This text is relatively IMMUTABLE, i.e. almost no other part of the game will try to change this, because it has mechanical consequences. Once a character Becomes something, it cannot Become anything else!
<br><br>
_Don't try to be everything, just try to be YOU. The more you are, the more the Deep Woods will change you._
